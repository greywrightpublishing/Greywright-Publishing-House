The Editor Behind Greywright

A note from the founder.

I started editing because I noticed how easily a manuscript's voice gets lost in the process, smoothed into something the author no longer recognizes as their own. Nearly five years and fifty-plus manuscripts later, that's still the line I won't cross.

Before I was an editor, I trained as a social worker, work that taught me to listen before I correct, and to treat someone's story as theirs to tell, not mine to rewrite. I bring that same instinct to every devotional, dissertation, memoir, and novel that crosses my desk.

Greywright exists so that instinct can scale beyond just me, without losing what made it worth scaling in the first place.
